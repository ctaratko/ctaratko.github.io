1. Introduction

AI Markets are a large part of today’s society, making its way as one of the largest industries in today’s financial markets. AI is a versatile too, that can be used both for large scale and small scale businesses, as well as in the hands of the consumer. There are multiple sectors and industries that are correlated to the AI industry, such as Tech, Energy, and Industrials/Manufacturing. These sectors are a very large portion of the pipeline of creating AI tools, infrastructure, and outcomes. Companies chosen to be researched within this project are companies like Nvidia, Microsoft, Meta, Oracle, Taiwan Semiconductor Metals, Equinix, and a variation of others. The chosen spread of companies to be analyzed is due to their relation to the AI industry, whether on the manufacturing end of Semiconductor metals ready to be used in creating the products, the manufacturers & architects of the units of compute (GPU, CPU, Motherboards, etc.), Data Center companies, or companies that heavily utilize the benefits of AI models / LLMs.

2. Literature Review

Three important Podcasts & Articles that built up the incentive to want to research this set of data came from sources like Wall Street Breakfast, How Money Works, and Time to Worry About AI’s Circular Deals (Smith, N, 2025, Asia Times). With the biases a few of these articles/podcasts contain, there is an underlying truth that relates there’s sources to macroeconomic influences in the market and how certain structures within companies can effect the market as a whole.

3. Data

The data was collected from the Quantmod R Package, an unofficial API that scrapes multiple financial platforms. In this case, the data came from Finance Yahoo, with the data being Daily values from 2014-01-01 till 2025-12-31. With that said, for the predictive nature, 2026+ data was overlain at the end to distinguish the current predictive nature of the model.

Variables utilized in this dataset are from both the standard data, as well as prepared data through cleaning & preparation:

Standard Data

  • Open

  • Close

  • High

  • Low

  • Adj. Close

  • Volume

Prepared Data

  • \(\alpha\) (Jensen’s Alpha \(\alpha\))

  • \(\beta\)

  • Sharpe Ratio

  • Treynor’s Ratio

The prepared data consists of important financial indicators of company and macro health of the economy. \(\alpha\) is the Excess Return against the index (or benchmark), while \(\beta\) is the Volatility of a stock compared to the overall market. For this set of data, the chosen benchmark for the Market was the SPY, specifically due to it’s market-capitalization-weighted-allocation, meaning the market-cap of the company determines how much the fund will allocate to it, but limited to S&P 500 companies only.

Data Visualizations

Company Correlation

Within these three models, consisting of 2 Pearson Correlation Maps & 1 Spearman Rank Correlation, the company with the highest correlation to most companies would be Nvidia, despite the large heat center among Google, Microsoft, Amazon, and Meta. Nvidia retains the highest average correlation to the most companies, and this can be attributed to its essential positioning between the industrial manufacturers and the end product infrastructure.

5. Unsupervised Learning Analysis

For the Unsupervised learning Analysis, the methods chosen were the PCA and K-Means Clustering. PCA. In the goal of trying to uncover harder-to-see trends within the list of companies, PCA allows us to figure out those smaller relationships that may have a larger impact than once believed. As for K-Means Clustering, it also performs a similar purpose by breaking down the data into different groups based on performance metrics, and slowly creates more and more distinction between the groupings.

PCA

Result Findings (PCA):

  • PC1 (The Market Factor): All values are solidly positive (blue), ranging from 0.20 to 0.36. This mathematical proof confirms that PC1 represents a rising tide lifting all stocks simultaneously (overall market or tech-sector direction).

  • PC2 (The SMCI Component): SMCI dominates this component with an exceptionally high loading of 0.73. This isolates the specific, independent upward volatility unique to SMCI seen in the previous biplot.

  • PC3 (The EQIX Contrast): EQIX has a heavy negative loading (-0.67), moving inversely to stocks like TSLA (0.40). This component captures days when data center infrastructure (EQIX) decoupled from electric vehicles/growth tech.

  • PC4 (The TSLA Outlier): TSLA holds an extreme negative weight of -0.75, showing that PC4 isolates trading days where Tesla’s performance diverged completely from the broader tech basket.

  • PC5 (SMCI vs. ORCL): This component captures a specific structural split between SMCI (0.58) and ORCL (-0.55).

Principal Component Analysis (PCA) successfully reduced the dimensionality of the 11-stock portfolio dataset while preserving structural information. The Scree Plot establishes that the first six principal components capture over 80% of the total dataset variance, with the first component (PC1) alone explaining 47.6%. This dominant PC1 axis functions mathematically as a macroeconomic “market factor,” as the Loadings Heatmap and Biplot reveal uniformly positive weights (0.20 to 0.36) across all tickers, indicating synchronized sector-wide movements. Subsequent components isolate unique, asset-specific risk and volatility decoupled from the broader market. Specifically, PC2 isolates idiosyncratic variance unique to SMCI (loading of 0.73), while PC3 and PC4 capture sharp, independent divergences in EQIX (-0.67) and TSLA (-0.75), respectively. Ultimately, this PCA framework simplifies the 11 original variables into a more compact set of orthogonal components that cleanly separate systematic market trends from unique individual stock anomalies.

6. Discussion / Implications

With the results of the model, the predictive nature has a lot of false positives in it’s supervised learning models, but does a great job at identifying company types and their key behaviors based on their market performance. The use of unsupervised and supervised machine learning models allow us to create an active strategy design in terms of being able to predict the most correlated stock’s movement, Nvidia.

While clustering maps historical structural boundaries, it assumes static market conditions. To project this portfolio into the future, we must test how these data-driven groups hold up under random, volatile market conditions.

The next section uses a Monte Carlo Simulation to run 10,000 randomized forward trials. This simulation leverages the exact covariance matrix and loading vectors established in our PCA step. By modeling random paths, we can transition from historical cluster descriptions to probabilistic future forecasting. This allows us to quantify the exact probability of achieving specific return targets while actively managing risk exposure across our three distinct stock clusters.

7. Conclusion

The overall output of this predictive modeling has been a very resourceful learning curve. The results of the Supervised and Unsupervised Learning models allowed the data to be sifted through, uncovering trends in the data such as seasonal and clustered groupings of companies. While the supervised learning produced sub-par results, with accuracy ranging between 47% to 54%, the ability to uncover trends in the data was the most meaningful part of this machine learning analysis. Being able to filter noise from general trends is a significant part of the data, allowing further models to be implemented, and to then break off more noise into potentially a well-structured analysis.

Overall, from my analysis of the data, the predictive nature of the analysis isn’t completely adequate for implementing as a near-guaranteed stock analysis, but it does show a good trend for the very short term (~15 to 30 days). This does show for a good baseline to start further analysis of stocks within this industry, but can definitely be further improved to produce either more accurate results in the short term, or a steadier trendline for future growth. Ideally, the harder of those two options would be to predict steady yet accurate trendlines for the long term, but short term would be ideal for improving market knowledge and specified accuracy of the prediction.
